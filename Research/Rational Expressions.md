# Multiplying, Dividing, Adding, and Subtracting [[Rational Expressions]]

### Reduce to lowest terms
Ex: Simplify $\frac{21}{45}$ by reducing to lowest terms. 

- 21 = 7 * 3, 45 = 5 * 3 * 3
>[!hint] $\frac{7 * 3}{5 * 3 * 3} = \frac{7}{15}$

Ex: Simplify $\frac{3x + 6}{x^2 + 4x + 4}$ by reducing to lowest terms. 

- Factor out 3 from the numerator, and then [[Foil]] the denominator
>[!hint] $\frac{3(x + 2)}{(x + 2)(x + 2)} = \frac{3}{x + 2}$

### Multiplying and Dividing
Ex: Compute    
a) $\frac{4}{3}*\frac{2}{5}$ 

- simply multiply the numerators and denominators
>[!hint] $\frac{4 * 2}{3 * 5} = \frac{8}{15}$

b) $\frac{\frac{4}{5}}{\frac{2}{3}}$

- multiply by the [[Reciprocal]] of the fraction in the denominator
>[!hint] $\frac{4}{5} * \frac{3}{2} = \frac{12}{10} = \frac{6}{5}$ 

Ex: Compute $\frac{\frac{x^2 + x}{x + 4}}{\frac{x + 1}{x^2-16}}$

- multiply by the [[Reciprocal]]
>[!hint] $\frac{x^2 + x}{x + 4} * \frac{x^2-16}{x+1} = \frac{(x^2+x)(x^2-16)}{(x+4)(x+1)}$

- Factor out x from $(x^2 + x)$ and factor [[Difference of Squares]] from $(x^2-16)$ 
>[!hint] $\frac{x(x+1)(x+4)(x-4)}{(x+4)(x+1)}$ Cancel Common Factors $= x(x-4)$

### Adding and Subtracting
Ex: Subtract $\frac{7}{6}-\frac{4}{15}$

- find [[LCD]](least common denominator)    6 = 2 * 3, 15 = 3 * 5, 2 * 3 * 5 = 30 (LCD)
- now multiply the denominators to make 30
>[!hint] $\frac{7}{6} * \frac{5}{5}$  and  $\frac{-4}{15} * \frac{2}{2} = \frac{35}{30} - \frac{8}{30} = \frac{27}{30} = \frac{9}{10}$

Ex: Add $\frac{3}{2x+2}+\frac{5}{x^2-1}$

- find LCD : $2x+2 = 2(x+1)$ and $x^2 = (x+1)(x-1)$ 
- therefore, LCD = $2(x+1)(x-1)$
>[!hint] $\frac{3}{2(x+1)}*\frac{(x-1)}{(x-1)} + \frac{5}{(x-1)(x+1)}*\frac{2}{2} = \frac{3(x-1)}{2(x+1)(x-1)} + \frac{5*2}{2(x+1)(x-1)} = \frac{3(x-1)+10}{2(x+1)(x-1)} = \frac{3x+7}{2(x+1)(x-1)}$
